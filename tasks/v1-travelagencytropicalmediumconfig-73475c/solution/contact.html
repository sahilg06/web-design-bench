Frequently Asked Questions

Don't see yours? Ask us directly — we usually reply within 24 hours.

How far in advance should I book?

For Explorer packages, 3–4 months out is comfortable. Premium and Ultimate trips are best booked 6–9 months ahead to lock in top guides and hotel rooms — especially for peak-season destinations like Santorini in July or Kyoto in cherry-blossom season.

What's included in the package price?

Flights, accommodation, guides, most meals (breakfast always; lunch/dinner counts listed on each package), all internal transport, entrance fees, and 24/7 support. What's not included: personal travel insurance on Explorer (included on Premium and Ultimate), tips for guides, and optional experiences you add along the way.

Can I change or cancel my trip?

Yes. Free cancellation and full refund up to 60 days before departure. Between 60 and 30 days, we hold your deposit as credit for 24 months. Under 30 days, cancellations depend on supplier terms — but we'll always try to help you rebook, and travel insurance covers most emergencies.

Do you handle visas and paperwork?

We prepare a personalised entry-requirements pack for every traveler, handle e-visas where possible free of charge, and can arrange more complex visa applications through our partner agencies for a small fee. You'll never have to guess what to submit.

Are your trips family-friendly?

Absolutely. About 40% of our travelers are multi-generational families. We build itineraries that flex to different energy levels — think grandparents pottering in a Kyoto tea house while teens learn to surf. Kids under 5 travel at 30% off on Explorer and Premium packages.

What about dietary needs and accessibility?

Every itinerary is pre-briefed with your dietary requirements — vegan, halal, gluten-free, allergies, whatever you need — and we cross-check every restaurant. For accessibility, tell us your specific needs (mobility, sensory, medical) on the inquiry form and our designer will build a trip that actually works, not one you have to fight through.
