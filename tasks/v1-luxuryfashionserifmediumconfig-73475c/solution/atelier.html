The Maison

A House Built by Hand

Maison Élégance was born in a fifth-floor studio on Rue Saint-Honoré, in the winter of 1987. Its founder, Camille Lévesque, had spent twenty-two years as première d'atelier at one of the great Parisian couture houses before deciding, at forty-one, that she preferred her own scissors.

The Maison she built refuses the pace of the industry. Two collections a year — never more. Every garment is cut, sewn, and finished in France, in an atelier of thirty-eight artisans who trained, without exception, at the Chambre Syndicale de la Haute Couture.

Chronology

Nearly Four Decades

1987

Founded on Rue Saint-Honoré

Camille Lévesque opens the first Maison Élégance atelier above the bookseller Delamain, with a staff of four seamstresses and a single Bernina 830.

1995

First Runway, Palais de Tokyo

The Printemps-Été 1995 collection debuts to critical acclaim, earning the Maison its first mention in French Vogue as “the quietest revolution in Paris.”

2003

Milan Boutique Opens

The Maison crosses the Alps. A restored 17th-century salon on Via della Spiga becomes the second flagship, doubling the Maison's presence in Europe.

2015

Métiers d'Excellence Award

Awarded by the French Ministry of Culture in recognition of the Maison's commitment to traditional couture craftsmanship and artisanal training programs.

2024

The Tokyo Salon

A fourth boutique opens in Ginza — a collaboration with the master textile studio Nishijin, marking the Maison's first Asian atelier partnership.

Métier

Materials of the House

Loro Piana Cashmere

Sourced from a single herd in Inner Mongolia, hand-combed at first snowfall.

Silk Duchesse of Como

Woven on 19th-century Jacquard looms in Lombardy — 62 grams per square metre.

Cuir de Vachette

Vegetable-tanned in the Tannerie Rémy Carriat, Normandy, over eight patient weeks.

Wool of Huddersfield

Super 180s worsted wool milled in Yorkshire since 1837 — the Maison's winter foundation.

The Hands

Portraits of the Atelier

Isabelle Rocher

Première d'Atelier · Flou

Thirty-one years at the Maison. Trained under Camille herself. Specialises in draped silk and asymmetric evening gowns.

Étienne Bourgeois

Master Tailor · Tailleur

A veteran of Savile Row who joined the Maison in 1998. His signature is the shoulder line — invisible, weightless, exact.

Yuki Nakamura

Head of Embroidery · Broderie

Formerly of the Lesage archives. Leads a team of six embroiderers whose work adorns every couture piece from the Maison.
